Hey there! My name is Todd Morrill.

I'm a second-year PhD student in Computer Science at Columbia University advised by Professor [Richard Zemel](https://www.cs.columbia.edu/~zemel/). During my master's in CS at Columbia, I was also advised by Professor [Kathleen McKeown](https://www.cs.columbia.edu/~kathy/). I am broadly interested in machine learning and neuroscience and am currently working on and thinking about spiking neural networks and unsupervised multimodal representation learning using alternatives to backpropagation.

I currently live in New York City with my wife [Emily](https://emilysaunders28.github.io/) and our sheepadoodle, Ziggy. Outside of work, I like to bike, hike, camp, ski, and cook. In a former life, I loved to study languages and still speak Mandarin Chinese well enough. I also worked in industry for 10 years as a machine learning engineer at [PwC](https://www.pwc.com/us/en/services/consulting/technology/emerging-technology.html) implementing machine learning systems for our clients.

All views expressed on this site are my own.
